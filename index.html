About

I graduated from Stony Brook University with a degree in mathematics in 2020. I did research with CATNIP Lab centered around machine learning and data visualization, as well as some research on low-dimensional topology with Oleg Viro.

I also enjoy digital art, science fiction, and philosophy. My favorite philosopher is Donna Haraway; I highly recommend The Companion Species Manifesto to anyone.

Currently, I do research at IGI TU Graz under the supervision of Wolfgang Maass. My research topics include learning and computation in biological networks, evolutionary and gradient-based training, and modelling biological data. Recently, I have been interested in optimizing our simulators using low-level CUDA code, and have improved the memory consumption of our experiments by a factor of 10.
